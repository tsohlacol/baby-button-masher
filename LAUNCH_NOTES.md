## Draft Reddit Post

**Title:** I built a free app that turns keyboard mashing into fireworks and piano notes so my toddler can go wild without breaking my computer

**Body:**

My toddler loves my keyboard. She grabs my finger, leads me to my desk, and slaps my chair to ask to play with it. I figured it would be more fun for her if it was more interactive.

So I built Baby Button Masher. It's a full-screen Windows app that:

* Reacts to her key mashing (speech, piano notes, fireworks, animal sounds)
* Blocks Win key, Alt+Tab, and other escape shortcuts at a low level (Ctrl+Alt+Del always works as a safety override)
* Has a "parent exit" that gets back to the main screen by solving a quick math problem
* Works across multiple monitors
* It's completely free and open source

https://github.com/tsohlacol/baby-button-masher

If you try it out, please submit any bugs or feature requests at https://github.com/tsohlacol/baby-button-masher/issues

Happy to answer questions if anyone else has a tiny keyboard gremlin at home.
